Paris vs. London

Many of the fashions depicted in the Journal des Dames et des Modes from 1797-1804 are strikingly radical and uniquely Parisian. Comparing the JDM to fashion plates from British journals printed in London reinforces these claims, but also shows commonalities between the two warring countries. The infographic below (click to enlarge) draws comparisons between JDM and two British journals:

  • Gallery of Fashion: Published from _ to _ by Nicholas Heideloff. Two plates featuring 3-4 figures appeared monthly with accompanying descriptions.
  • Magazine of Fashions of London and Paris: Published from _ to _. Monthly...#figures. Almost every month included a plate titled "Parisian Fashions" that was a translation of plates from JDM

It is notable that these two journals only depicted women's fashions during this period, never men, and figures rarely appeared alone. Even when the Fashions of London and Paris copied JDM plates, they put two or more figures on the page - it would be highly improper for a woman to be out and about on her own!
